Player of the week

WTA player for 4/15 to 4/21/24: Sloane Stephens

Sloane is a former U.S. Open champion and is very talented. This year, she hasn't had that much success on the singles court this season, but made the final of the Charleston Open in doubles the week before. In this tournament, Sloane was seeded 6th. In the 1st round, she beat fellow American Peyton Stearns 1-6,6-1,6-3. In the second round, she beat Karolina Pliskova of the Czech Republic 6-3,6-2. In the quarter final, she beat 4th seeded Yue Yuan of China 6-2,6-2. In the semi-finals, she beat number 2 seeded Caroline Garcia of France 6-3,6-2. In the final, she beat unseeded Magda Linette of Poland 6-1,2-6,6-2. Overall, this week has helped Sloane gain confidence and hopefully, she can have an amazing clay court season.

ATP player for 4/15 to 4/21/24: Jan Lennard Struff

Jan-Lennard has been on the tour for a while and is 33 years old. During his time on tour, he has made 4 ATP singles finals, but hasn't won one. This week Jan-Lennard was seeded 4 in singles and was unseeded in doubles. Jan Lennard made the final of both events and won the singles. On his route to the singles title, he beat Holger Rune of Denmark, Felix Auger Aliassime of Canada, Taylor Fritz of the U.S., and Botic Van de Zandschulp of the Netherlands. Jan-Lennard became the 3rd oldest to win his first ever ATP singles title. Overall, this week was a turning point for Struff and was even more special that it was in his home country.
