Player of the week

WTA player to 3/17 to 3/24/24: Angella Okutoyi

Angella is from Kenya and has already achieved so much at just 20 years old. She became the 1st Kenyan to win at junior grand slam match, she did this at the Australian Open. She also became the 1st Kenyan to ever win a junior grandslam and she did this by winning the junior girls doubles. Angella currently plays divison 1 tennis at Auburn University. In the African Games which were played in Accra, Ghana, Angella ended up winning the gold medal and securing in a place at the Olympics in Paris. The only downside is that Angella has to be in the top 400 in the world by June to play the event. Okutoyi defeated Egypt's Lamis Alhussein Abdel Aziz 6-4, 6-2 in the final to back up a career-best win over Alhussein Abdel Aziz's compatriot, No.1 seed Mayar Sherif, in the semifinals. Okutoyi had trailed No.70-ranked Sherif by a set and 5-2, but battled back to triumph 5-7, 7-5, 7-6(5) in 4 hours and 27 minutes. Her gold medal run, was the first by a Kenyan woman at the African Games since Jane Davies-Doxzon in 1978.
