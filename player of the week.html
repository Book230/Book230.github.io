Player of the week

WTA player to 1/14 to 1/28/24 : Dayana Yastremska

Dayana had a wonderful run to the semi finals of the Australian Open. Additionally, she was just the 5th qualifier in the open era to make it that far in any of the grand slams. Dayana barely qualified to for the main draw , but didn't. Instead she had to play qualies as the number one seed. In qualies all her matches went to 3 sets. In the 1st round, she played Lea Boskovic of Croatia in 3 sets. In the second round, she beat Valentini Grammatikopoulou of Greece in 3 sets. In the final round of qualies she beat Australian wildcard Maya Joint in 3 sets. In the 1st round of the main draw, she upset the number 7 seed Marketa Vondrousova of the Czech Republic in straight sets. In the 2nd round, she beat Varvara Gracheva of France in straight sets. In the third round set got pushed to 3 sets by 27th seed Emma Navarro of the U.S. In the 4th round, she beat 18th seeded Victoria Azarenka of Belarus in straight sets. In the quarter finals she beat Linda Noskova an up and coming star from the Czech Republic who beat number 1 seeded Iga Swiatek in straight sets. In the semi finals, she would end up losing in straight sets to Qinwen Zheng on China, Zheng would end up losing to Sabalenka in the final. Overall, this tournament was a confidence booster for Yastreska because she had faced a ot of adversities such as the war with Ukraine and Russia and was put on a doping suspension.

ATP player for 1/14 to 1/28/24 : Jannik Sinner

Jannik Sinner became the only man to win the Australian Open from Italy and the first to win a grand slam in roughly 50 years. Sinner made history his country and it was his first grand slam ever. In doing so, Sinner beat 5 seeds, 3 in which were part of the top 5 seeds. In the semi finals he played world number one Novak Djokovic who has an incredible winning record at the Australian open and hasn't lost a match there since 2018. Sinner would end up beating him in 4 sets. In the final, Sinner came back from 2 sets down to Medvedev to beat him in 5 sets. This result showed Sinners dominance and how well he has been playing.
